Humidity

  • Measured in Relative Humidity percentage (% RH).
  • Indicates water vapor concentration (from 0% RH to 100% RH) in the air compared to the maximum amount it can hold at a specific temperature (saturated air has 100% humidity).
  • When humidity is near 100% RH, water vapor condenses and forms fog near the surface (or clouds in the sky).

Normal

Barometric Pressure

  • Measures air pressure on Earth’s surface in Pascals (air’s force on a square meter).
  • Range: 30,000Pa to 110,000Pa (Altitude Range: 0 to 30,000 ft).
  • 101,325 Pa is the average pressure at sea-level and fluctuates depending on weather conditions.
  • The higher altutide has lower air pressure, leading to altitude sickness due to the lack of oxygen in the air.

High

VOCs

  • Measures the amount of Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) in the air in parts per billion (ppb).
  • Range: 0 to 32,768 parts per billion.
  • VOCs are pollutants and/or sensory irritants from trees, animals, constructions,...
  • Although VOCs are not used to calculate Air Quality Index, they are helpful to predict photochemical smog. .
  • High levels of VOCs (tVOC over 5 ppb) can affect sensitive people (Source).Exposing to air with tVOC over 100ppb in long-term can cause nasal cancer (Source).
